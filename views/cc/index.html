The how & why of adding
CC support to our videos

What is closed captioning?

Captioning is the process of displaying text overlayed on video to provide additional orinterpretive information.

Closed (versus "Open") indicates that the captions are not visible until activated by the viewer.

Why we should add CC support

  • Captioning video creates accessible content for individuals who are deaf or hearing impaired.
  • Captions are also important for anyone working in a noisy enviroment.
  • Captions add another dimension to video content, making a richer learning experience for students who have specific neurological processing problems, non-native speakers, adults working toward basic literacy, and children learning to read.
  • Google indexes captions in video files, making the content accessible to search engines as well.

How do we add captions to our videos?

We can transcribe the videos ourselves and create a SubRip (SRT) file which matches the text display to the video playback time.
